INTRODUCTION TO THE QUANTUM WORLD

Quantum physics is about probabilites: the probability that some form of matter will be in one place or another at any given moment. And that position is mutually dependant on momentum. We will only which position and momentum the particle has once it has been observed. Before that, it has all positions and all momenta.

That is called the superposition.
And that is revolutionary.

If you think you understand quantum mechanics, you don't understand quantum mechanics.

RICHARD FEYNMAN

QUANTUM SOCIAL MEDIA

Ok, the title of this section may be slightly misleading, but i promise you there is good content ahead!

Due to quantum physics being a very new and relatively unexplored field (compared to classical physics), there is always new discoveries and content being published. One of the most brilliant places to find this content is with the releases of academic papers. arXiv.org archive these papers, with content that ranges from physics to mathematics, finance, computer science and more. But most relevant, is that they regularly update with new releases of papers, posting a tweet when they do. The twitter feed for arXiv.org can be found on here.
